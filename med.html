Background

Recently Whatsapp invited entrepreneurs to participate in the WhatsApp-Startup India Grand challenge. Entrepreneurs with highly innovative ideas, business models that solve for a local India problem, making a large scale socio-economic impact, were invited to apply.

For this challenge I came across an interesting problem faced by many in India. The youth and some adults there find it highly uncomfortable to purchase condoms at their local pharmacies.

Research & Discovery

Primary research was focused on a thorough competetive analysis. This analysis provided information on what options are currently available to the users and who the key players in the market are. Secondary research - user interviews helped get a deeper understanding of the problem faced by the users themselves - "What made the experience uncomfortable?".

Persona

The next step was to answer the most important question - "Who am I desiging for?" Since the goal was to design user centric produts, it was absolutely essential to get a deep understanding of our target audience, their motivations and requirements. I charted out character traits, behaviours of our potential users.

User Flow

Mapping out the user flow helped in visualizing the number of steps a user needs to complete specific task. It also gave an insight into the architecture of the app.

Wireframes

Once I had a clear vision of all the required screens I began translating them into rough sketches. I started out with the classic pen and paper route. Drawing boxes out helped in getting a better understanding of different layouts. I later converted them into low - fidelty Wireframes using Sketch.

Design + Prototype

Once wireframe was finalized the next step was to add visual design. I created a style guide which I could use as reference. It helped keeping things symmetric and also made the design process much faster. The final design was made in Sketch with the prototyping done in Principle. You can see the final app below.
